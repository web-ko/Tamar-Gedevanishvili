BIOGRAPHY OF THE ARTIST

Tamar Gedevanishvili was born on March 1, 1969, in Tbilisi, Georgia.

EDUCATION:

1988−89: Karaman Kutateladze's workshop.
1989−95: Tbilisi State Academy of Art, initially enrolled in the Faculty of Architecture, and later pursued studies in the Faculty of Graphic Design.

WORK:

Since 2021, she has been serving as an associate professor at GIPA (Institute of Public Affairs of Georgia), specializing in "Drawing," visual communication/advertising, and communication design.
Since 2002 he has been working as a graphic designer in an advertising companies.

EXHIBITIONS:

Tamar held her first solo exhibition in 1987 at the Merani Publishing House.

In June 2022, Tamar presented a solo exhibition titled "Improper Intersections" at the Museum of History, Karvasla. The exhibition, curated by Lika Mamatsashvili, featured a collection of acrylic paintings and graphic works.

In 2023, Tamar actively participated in the "Free City" project, a collaborative effort by Georgian artists in solidarity with the Ukrainian people.

On May 6, 2023, she showcased an art performance titled "Connections," dedicated to Europe Day and implemented by the European Union and United Nations Development Program. The polyptych presentation was one of the highlighted works at the Expo Georgia Pavilion, curated by Khatuna Khabuliani.

From June 8 to 12, 2023, Tamar was part of the "Our Times" group exhibition, organized by the Kettari Foundation and curated by Khatuna Khabuliani and Elichka Nadareishvili.

Closing the year, from December 19 to January 14, Tamar contributed to the joint exhibition "Holiday/Fiesta" at n9Gallery, curated by Maka Iovadze. The exhibition featured three works from the "Improper Intersections" series, including a 4−part panel measuring 200x140 cm and two additional works sized at 42x30 cm, all created using paper and acrylic.

2024, 19 April–10 May – group exhibition of modern Georgian Artists in Kutaisi at David Kakabadze Fine Art Gallery. Curators Eliso Chogovadze, Mamia Tkeshelashvili. Were presented two works – 1. graphic from the series “For a Whole Life“ 21x30 cm (paper, pen), 2. From the series “Improper Intersections”, 80x60 cm (paper, acrylic)

"I thought it was a palace, fountains and gardens, peacocks on mowed lawns, everything luxurious and beautiful... and where did that palace disappear? Maybe the peacock got sick and lost its tail, and the fountain got old and covered with moss. The stormy sea and the ruins..."
